## What Is a QR Code (and Why You Need One)?

A **QR code** is a two-dimensional barcode that encodes information (URLs, text, WiFi, contact details, payment links) into a square pattern of black and white dots. When scanned with a smartphone camera, it instantly opens the destination—no app download needed.

**Why QR codes matter:**
- **Simple:** One scan connects offline to online instantly
- **Versatile:** Works for URLs, WiFi passwords, contact cards, payment links, and more
- **Trackable:** Dynamic QR codes show you exactly how many people scanned and when
- **Accessible:** 99.5 million people in the US are expected to scan QR codes in 2025
- **Cost-free:** No recurring fees, no physical materials needed

## Static vs. Dynamic QR Codes: What's the Difference?

Understanding QR code types helps you choose the right tool for your goal.

| Type | What It Does | Best For | Editing | Cost |
|------|-------------|----------|---------|------|
| **Static** | Fixed destination; cannot be changed after creation | URLs, WiFi passwords, contact cards (one-time use) | No | Free forever |
| **Dynamic** | Editable destination; update URL anytime without reprinting | Marketing campaigns, seasonal promos, A/B testing | Yes | Free (2 codes) or paid |

**Real-world use:** A retail business uses dynamic QR codes on flyers. If they want to change the landing page next month, they update the code's target—no new print materials needed.

## Best QR Code Types for Different Uses

**URL QR Code**  
Direct users to a landing page, blog post, or online store. Most common for marketing.

**WiFi QR Code**  
Guests scan to connect to your network instantly. No password needed after first scan.

**vCard (Contact) QR Code**  
Share your contact details instantly. Recipients can save your name, phone, email, and address in one tap.

**Email QR Code**  
Pre-fills an email with subject line and recipient. Great for event sign-ups and customer feedback.

**PDF QR Code**  
Link to a downloadable brochure, menu, or guide. Reduces paper waste and tracks engagement.

**App Store QR Code**  
Direct users straight to your app on Apple App Store or Google Play. Boosts downloads from packaging, posters, or ads.

## Common QR Code Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

**Too small to scan.**  
Minimum size is 2 cm × 2 cm (about 0.8 inches). If it looks tiny on your print material, enlarge it.

**No call-to-action.**  
Users won't scan something they don't understand. Add text like "Scan for menu" or "Get your discount here."

**Poor placement.**  
Place QR codes in high-contrast areas (white background, no glare). Avoid corners or edges where they might get cut off.

**Ignoring analytics.**  
Use dynamic QR codes to track scans. See which campaigns work, which don't, and optimize next time.

# C) FAQ

**Q: Are QR codes safe?**  
A: Yes, if generated from a reputable source. Avoid scanning codes from unknown sources, as they could redirect to phishing sites. Use codes you create yourself or from trusted generators.

**Q: Can I edit a QR code after I print it?**  
A: Only dynamic QR codes allow edits. Static codes are fixed forever once printed. If you might change the destination, use dynamic.

**Q: How many times can a QR code be scanned?**  
A: Unlimited. A QR code never expires; it can be scanned thousands of times.

**Q: Can I customize the design of my QR code?**  
A: Yes. Most generators let you add logos, colors, and custom frames. Keep the center 30% of the code clear for scannability.

**Q: What's the difference between static and dynamic?**  
A: Static codes are fixed and free forever. Dynamic codes can be edited and offer analytics, but often require a paid plan for multiple codes.
